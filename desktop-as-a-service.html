Desktop as a Service (DaaS) is a cloud computing solution that securely delivers virtual apps and desktops to any device, anywhere. This fully managed virtualization service supports both secure SaaS and legacy applications, as well as complete Windows-based virtual desktops, ensuring your workforce stays connected and productive. With a predictable pay-as-you-go subscription model, DaaS lets you scale up or down on demand. It’s simple to manage, reduces IT administration, and streamlines desktop operations.

How does DaaS work?

Desktop as a Service (DaaS) delivers virtual applications and desktop environments through a public or private cloud. Employees can securely connect via an HTML-based browser or a dedicated app on laptops, desktops, thin clients, smartphones, or tablets.

Offered as a subscription and built on a multitenant model, DaaS is powered by backend Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI) hosted by a third-party cloud provider. The provider streams virtual desktops to end-user devices while managing infrastructure, security, upgrades, backups, and storage. Organizations retain control over their applications and desktop images, making DaaS an ideal option for those who want enterprise-grade desktops without the cost and complexity of running their own on-premises VDI.

Why DaaS?

Today’s employees expect to work from anywhere, on any device. Businesses need a cost-effective, straightforward way to securely deliver the apps and desktops their teams rely on. Traditional PC environments bring constant IT headaches, from routine password resets to complex security patches and updates, that consume valuable administrator time, degrade user experience, and slow productivity.

While virtualization offers benefits, it often demands significant capital investment and specialized expertise. That’s why many organizations are turning to Desktop as a Service (DaaS). DaaS delivers the centralization, security, and control of VDI, but with the simplicity of having a trusted provider handle desktop management, shifting costs to a predictable operational expense model.

Secure

Centralized data storage ensures information is kept on secure servers rather than individual devices, reducing risks of loss, theft, or unauthorized access. Centralized backups enable fast, efficient data recovery in the event of disasters or hardware failures.

VDI environments provide enhanced security through encryption, granular access controls, and regular updates. With integration to Identity-Aware Proxy (IAP), or Active Directory, you can enforce robust authentication and authorization. Encrypted desktop streaming ensures secure access for employees, no matter where they work.

Cost-effective

VDI helps cut hardware costs by enabling older or less powerful devices to run virtual desktops, eliminating frequent upgrade cycles. Centralized management simplifies maintenance, making patching, updates, and troubleshooting more efficient while reducing IT overhead.

Scalable by design, VDI can quickly adjust to changing business needs, adding or removing desktops as required. With partner ecosystem, you get a best-fit solution running on infrastructure that scales rapidly and cost-effectively.

Productive

VDI delivers a consistent, standardized desktop experience across devices, minimizing compatibility issues and ensuring a familiar work environment for all users. New employees can be onboarded faster with ready-to-go virtual desktops, while centralized maintenance reduces downtime and keeps teams productive. By migrating and replatforming applications to the cloud, businesses can boost agility, simplify operations, and respond quickly to evolving demands.
